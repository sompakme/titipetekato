Ryan Ashley Biography, Age

Ryan Ashley Malarkey was born on April 29, 1987, in northeastern Pennsylvania. Nothing is known of her before her higher education. Since she was little, Ashley has always been in love with art. Years later, she chose to pursue this dream in the world of fashion design.

Ryan Ashley enrolled at the Fashion Institute of Technology to study fashion design. She graduated in 2007, winning her class’s Critics’ Choice award. After graduation, she was hired by a private label clothing company where she worked as an artist and fashion designer until 2011.

Ryan had decided to pursue his pursuit of art a little differently – as a tattoo artist. She returned to her home state of Pennsylvania and apprenticed at a private tattoo studio, where she learned the ropes of the business and began working on her unique design style. She spent a year there.

Ashley has since developed an entrepreneurial side to her tattoo talent. She has her own private, appointment-based tattoo studio in the city of Kingston, Pennsylvania. Ryan is also the co-owner of an e-commerce platform where she sells bizarre shitty items, from glass eyes and skulls to bath bombs and tarot cards.

Ashley entered the 8th installment of the Masters of Ink tattoo artist contest in 2016 and came out on top. This makes her the first woman to win the contest since its inception. The following year, Ryan Ashley starred on Ink Masters: Angels, which was a spinoff of the original she won in 2016. On the show, she toured the country with Gia Rose, Kelly Doty and Nikki Simpson, who were all contestants with Ashley on Ink Masters.

In this spin-off show, Ryan and his former contestants traveled across the country and held competitions in different cities for up-and-coming and local tattoo artists pitting them against each other. It was designed so that the winner of the contest could face Ashley or one of her friends. The Ink Masters: Angels also doubled as a means of recruiting candidates for the following season of the original Ink Masters competition. She continued her role as host of the spin-off show with her return for its second season in March 2018.

Ryan Ashley has also been on the cover of various body art publications such as the June/July 2015 issues of Inked Magazine and Freshly Inked.
