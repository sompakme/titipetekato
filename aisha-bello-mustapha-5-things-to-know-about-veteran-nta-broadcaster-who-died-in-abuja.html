FCT, Abuja - A famous Nigeria Television Authority News (NTA) presenter, Aisha Bello Mustapha, has reportedly passed on after a brief illness in Abuja.

The presenter was a popular face on Nigerian television in the 90s and early 2000s until her retirement in 2022, BBC Pidgin reported.

As reported by PM News, Zainab Sabo, a friend of Aisha's family, confirmed the death and added that her funeral prayer will take place at Abuja Central Mosque by 1pm on Monday, December 11, according to Muslim rites.

“It's true that Mama (Aisha) is dead and her funeral prayer is slated for 1:00pm. We will really miss her and we pray for God to bless her soul.” Zainab told BBC Pidgin.

Facts about Aisha Bello Mustapha

Following her death, Legit.ng gathered a brief profile of the late Aisha Bello Mustapha.

When did Aisha Bello Mustapha retire?

After 35 years of working with the Nigeria Television Authority (NTA), Aisha retired in May 2022. This concluded her remarkable career in the broadcasting industry.

Former GM of NTA Parliament

The late Aisha was versatile; her skill set included journalism, television production, broadcasting, and editing. Her last role was the general manager of NTA Parliament before retiring.

A popular TV personality

Her professional manner and unique style solidified her as one of NTA's most popular faces and voices, leaving a big mark in the Nigerian broadcast industry.

Aisha, a veteran broadcaster

Aisha's legacy in the Nigerian media was huge. She started her journey at NTA, rising to the role of an assistant director of news and later becoming the general manager of NTA Parliament. She was one of the popular broadcasters on NTA Network News during the '90s and early 2000s.

What did Aisha Bello Mustapha study?

Meanwhile, the late veteran broadcaster earned a Bachelor's degree in Mass Communication/Media studies from Ahmadu Bello University (ABU), Zaria, located in Kaduna state.
