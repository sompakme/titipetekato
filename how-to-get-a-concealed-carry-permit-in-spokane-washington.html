So You Wanna Be a Spokane Spoiler? Your Guide to Getting a Concealed Carry Permit (CCP)

Alright, citizens of Spokane, let's talk turkey. You've seen a rise in, well, let's say spirited squirrel activity in your neighborhood. Maybe that pesky raccoon keeps eyeing your prize-winning begonias with a little too much interest. Or, perhaps you're just a responsible citizen who believes in the right to bear arms... squirrel bops notwithstanding. Whatever your reason, you're here for one thing: a Concealed Carry Permit (CCP) in Spokane, Washington.

But hold your horses (or should we say, holster your holsters?) Getting a CCP isn't exactly like picking up a latte at your favorite drive-thru. There are a few hoops to jump through, but fear not, my friend! This guide will have you navigating the permitting process smoother than a greased pig at a county fair.

Step 1: Unleash Your Inner Legal Eagle (or at least read some legalese)

First things first, Spokane County issues CCPs, so crack open a beverage of choice (preferably non-alcoholic, we'll get to that later) and brush up on the requirements. You can be a Spokane County resident, 21 years or older, and you'll need to be a squeaky-clean citizen with no major disqualifying offenses on your record. Think misdemeanors involving domestic violence or felonies – that sort of thing is a big no-no.

Step 2: Channel Your Inner Sherlock Holmes (minus the deerstalker hat)

Alright, detective time! You'll need to undergo some training. Don't worry, it's not like CIA spy school (although that would be pretty darn cool). Look for a Washington State approved Concealed Pistol License (CPL) training course. These classes will teach you the fundamentals of firearm safety and legalities of concealed carry.

Step 3: Get Your Fingerprint Groove On (Because Criminals Don't Have Hobbies)

Background check time! You'll need to be fingerprinted, which is pretty standard procedure. Don't worry, they're not taking your prints to dust for past crimes (though, maybe they should check for past bakery heists – who can resist a good donut?). The Spokane County Sheriff's Office or Spokane Valley Precinct offer fingerprinting services by appointment.

Step 4: Apply Online and Unleash Your Inner Accountant (Sort Of)

The fun part (well, maybe not as fun as firing off a round at the range)! You can apply for your CCP online through the Spokane Police Department's snazzy online portal. There's a fee involved, so be prepared to loosen your purse strings a bit. Remember, responsible gun ownership doesn't come cheap.

Step 5: Patience, Grasshopper (Because Bureaucracy Takes Time)

Once you've submitted your application, it's time to twiddle your thumbs and wait. Processing times can vary, so don't get discouraged if you don't hear back immediately. Just channel your inner zen master and focus on your breathing.

Congratulations! You're Now a Spokane Spoiler (But With Great Responsibility)

There you have it, folks! With a little perseverance and maybe a few online rabbit holes about responsible gun ownership (because knowledge is power!), you'll be sporting your CCP with pride. Just remember, with great power comes great responsibility. Always follow gun safety protocols, be mindful of where you carry, and for the love of all things huckleberry, don't use your new permit to rob a donut shop.

FAQs:

How to find a CPL training course?

A quick Google search for "Washington State CPL training course" should do the trick.

How long does the CCP application process take?

Processing times can vary, but it typically takes a few weeks.

How much does a CCP cost?

There is a fee associated with the application, so check the Spokane Police Department's website for current rates.

Do I need a permit to open carry in Spokane?

No, Washington State is an open carry state, but a CCP allows you to carry a concealed firearm.

Where can I find more information about CCPs in Spokane?

The Spokane Police Department website has a wealth of information on CCPs: https://my.spokanecity.org/police/information/licenses-and-permits/
