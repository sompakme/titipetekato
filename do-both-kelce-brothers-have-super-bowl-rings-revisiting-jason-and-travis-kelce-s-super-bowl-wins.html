Do both Kelce brothers have Super Bowl rings? Revisiting Jason and Travis Kelce's Super Bowl wins

 Posted on September 17, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  1006 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Brothers Jason and Travis Kelce will make history when they play in the 2023 Super Bowl. It will be the first time a pair of siblings will square off against each other in the big game in less than two weeks time.

While Travis will suit up for the Kansas City Chiefs, Jason will don the colors for the Philadelphia Eagles. The brothers already have one Super Bowl ring each, but this year's Super Bowl will give one of them the bragging rights in the family.

Here, we take a look back at when Jason and Travis won their respective Super Bowl rings.

When did Travis Kelce win the Super Bowl?

Travis Kelce won the Super Bowl with the Chiefs in 2020. Kansas City took down the San Francisco 49ers 31-20 in the final game of the season to claim the title.

The tight end recorded 43 yards on seven receptions with one touchdown against the Niners to help the Chiefs win the Championship.

When did Jason Kelce win the Super Bowl?

Jason Kelce won the Super Bowl with the Eagles in 2018 when his team beat the New England Patriots 41-33. At the time, quarterback Tom Brady was leading the Patriots' offense.

The center later grabbed headlines when he gave an impassioned speech where he defended his teammates, coaches, and front office during the Eagles' title parade.

Will Travis Kelce give a better Super Bowl speech than Jason Kelce?

Jason Kelce gave Philadelphia fans a speech for the ages after the Eagles clinched the Super Bowl. His brother Travis followed suit when the Chiefs lifted the Lombardi Trophy in 2020.

Rallying victory speeches run in the Kelce family. However, as older brother Jason was the first to initiate the victory speech, it doesn't seem like his Travis could do anything to top it if the Chiefs emerge victorious on Sunday.

Is Travis Kelce better than Jason Witten?

Many fans will argue that Jason Witten is one of the best tight ends to have played in the NFL. However, the 11-time Pro Bowler never won a Super Bowl title.

Travis Kelce, on the other hand, won a Super Bowl in 2020. The Chiefs star also has better stats in comparison to Witten in postseason games.

Witten recorded 486 receiving yards on 45 receptions and caught one touchdown in eight playoff games. Kelce has 993 receiving yards on 83 receptions and nine touchdowns across 12 games in the postseason.

Super Bowl 2023: When, where, and how to watch?

The 2023 Super Bowl is bound to be a thrilling spectacle. Two of the best teams from each conference, the Chiefs and the Eagles, will lock horns on Sunday, February 12.

The game will be played at the State Farm Stadium in Glendale, Arizona, with kick-off at 6:30 pm ET. Fans can watch the Super Bowl live on Fox. Those who don't have access to cable can stream the event on Fox's website.
