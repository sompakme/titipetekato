How do Italian friends greet each other?

 Posted on August 8, 2024  |  6 minutes  |  1161 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon
The common verbal greeting is “Ciao” (Hello). This is quite casual. People may also say “Buongiorno” (Good day) or “Buonasera” (Good evening) to be more formal. Address a person by their title and last name, and continue to do so until invited to move to a first-name basis.

How do friends greet each other in Italy?

When meeting new people, Italians greet them by shaking hands. They use two kisses (first on the right cheek and second on the left cheek) or a hug with friends (amici) they've known for a long time.
