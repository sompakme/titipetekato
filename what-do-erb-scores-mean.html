What exactly does a percentile rank mean?

In computing a score’s percentile rank, we consider the proportion of scores in its frequency distribution that are equal to or lower than the score in question. If a test score is higher than 75 percent of the scores obtained by those who took the test, the score will be referred to as being in the 75th percentile, where 75 represents the percentile rank.

What is the best way to understand percentile rankings?

Percentile “ranks” are a kind of ranking system. If you “scored in the 66th percentile,” this means that you performed “as well as or better than” 66 percent of the other participants in the group. It is considered to be in the 50th percentile if your result was the same as “the mean” for that particular exam. In statistics, the mean is the midpoint of a distribution, and the 50th percentile is the same as the mean of a group of people.

What exactly does the grade Stanine denote?

For example, a stanine score of 1, 2, or 3 indicates that the person is below average; 4, 5, or 6 indicates that the person is average; and 7, 8, or 9 indicates that the person is above average.
