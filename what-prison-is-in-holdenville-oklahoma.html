The Big House of Holdenville: Unveiling Oklahoma's Not-So-Secret Prison

So you're curious about the clink in Holdenville, Oklahoma? Maybe you're planning a visit (to your long-lost pen pal, obviously), or perhaps you're just feeding your fascination with all things correctional. Whatever your reason, you've come to the right place! But before we spill the beans, let's dispel a few myths.

  • Myth #1: It's a vacation resort with bars (on the windows, that is).
  • Myth #2: Conjuring a comfy cot and gourmet meals will land you a stay.
  • Reality: It's a correctional facility, folks. But hey, at least they have cable (probably... maybe...).

Introducing the Allen Gamble Correctional Center (Formerly Davis Correctional Facility)

This correctional facility, with a name change as dramatic as a prison escape (more on that later), houses a hefty population of inmates. We're talking medium and maximum security under one roof, which is like combining a kitten daycare with a lion enclosure (although hopefully a little less chaotic).

Fun Fact: Did you know the prison was renamed in honor of a fallen correctional officer? Now that's some serious respect.

So, You Want to Know More About the Allen Gamble Correctional Center?

Alright, alright, enough suspense. Here's the juicy stuff:

  • Who runs the joint? The Oklahoma Department of Corrections calls the shots, but the day-to-day operations are handled by CoreCivic.
  • How big is this place? We're talking enough room for over 1700 inmates, which is like a never-ending game of prison tag.
  • Can I visit someone there? Absolutely! But be prepared for some security checks that would make Fort Knox jealous.

Word to the wise: Check their website for the latest visitation protocols. Trust us, nobody wants a hangry wait in line after getting denied entry for forgetting Aunt Mildred's fruitcake recipe (prison rules, people).

How to Avoid a One-Way Trip to the Allen Gamble Correctional Center

Let's be honest, prison isn't exactly a five-star experience. So, here are some helpful tips to keep you on the right side of the law:

  • Don't jaywalk. Just because the pigeons do it, doesn't mean you should.
  • Resist the urge to rob a bank (seriously, it's a bad idea).
  • Maybe skip that midnight skinny-dipping session in the town fountain. Public indecency is a frown-worthy offense.

Frequently Asked Questions (Because Let's Face It, You Have Them)

How to find the Allen Gamble Correctional Center? Easy! Google Maps is your friend, but for a more thrilling experience, try a treasure hunt with clues like "follow the barbed wire fence." (We don't recommend this, but hey, to each their own).

How to contact the Allen Gamble Correctional Center? Their website is a good starting point, but for a more personal touch, carrier pigeons might be faster than their phone lines. (Again, not recommended).

How to become a guard at the Allen Gamble Correctional Center? Check their website for job openings. But be warned, dealing with interesting characters is pretty much a guarantee.

How to send money to an inmate at the Allen Gamble Correctional Center? There are specific procedures, so be sure to consult their website. Just remember, they probably won't be able to afford caviar with your donation.

How to break someone out of the Allen Gamble Correctional Center? We strongly advise against this. Not only is it illegal, but it's also a recipe for disaster (and a lifetime movie).
